PORTRAIT · CHEF
Chef David Tsan

David Tsan apprenticed for nine years between Tokyo and Hokkaido before bringing a single eight-seat counter to East End Market. He buys the day's fish himself, breaks it himself, and writes the night's menu in pencil an hour before the doors open.

"The counter is small on purpose. I want to remember every face I serve, and I want every plate to look like the only one I made tonight."

The room is built around one ritual: hand to plate to guest, no waiters between. There is no printed menu. There are no two nights alike. The fish decides.

— D. TSAN, ITAMAE
RITUAL · 01 RESERVATION
Omakase
"I leave it to you."

A seventeen-course tasting at the cedar counter. Two hours. Eight seats. The chef builds the meal around what arrived that morning — nigiri pressed in front of you, small kappo plates between, finished with a bowl of tamago that has been the same recipe since 2016.
