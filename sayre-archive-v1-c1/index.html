The bottle · archive specimen · batch I

At Avignon the rooms cool before the streets do. We worked in that hour: copper still warm, windows blackening, fig leaves drying flat under linen. The first tincture was not sweet. It carried the green snap of the branch.

Iris arrived slowly, a mineral powder in the register, then smoke. Not the theatrical smoke of a room made for being seen, but the residue left in wool after a candle has gone out. We kept the measurement marks because the scent resisted adjectives.

Sayre № 01 is bottled as a primary source. No decoration beyond the necessary number. No promise beyond the pressure of dusk, held briefly in amber glass.

“The page argues that fragrance is both: a felt thing and a measured thing.”
