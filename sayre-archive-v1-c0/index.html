The Story · Autumn 2026

Distilled in a stone room in Avignon, by the kind of light that only arrives in November — low, amber, late. The window is small. The copper is older than the building.

Inside the bottle: smoked iris, dried tobacco leaf, the resin of a tree that no longer grows nearby. A perfume that smells like a room, not a person.

Bottled in stone-coloured glass, sealed by hand, numbered by ink. Three hundred bottles, then nothing again until spring.
